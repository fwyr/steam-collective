Who are we?

The Dunman High STEAM Collective is a vibrant and inclusive space for youths who are keen to work with science, technology, engineering, arts, and mathematics.

Divided into Infocomm and Robotics Chapters, students receive opportunities to develop their skills in various sectors of coding and design, such as competitive programming, robotics, and web development.

We aim to:

Nurture future-ready leaders in STEAM domains.

Hone interdisciplinary thinking by exposing students to various STEAM domains, complete with workshops and talks.

Broaden mechanical knowledge and allow students to see technology as both a hobby and a career path.

Have fun.

Burning questions? We've quelled some.

What are the Infocomm and Robotics Chapters?

The Infocomm Chapter places a greater emphasis on software development, website design and algorithmic thinking, whereas the Robotics Chapter works with physics, robot construction, and hardware engineering.

In Year 1, students will learn the basics of programming and engineering. From Year 2 onwards, club members will make a choice to join either the Infocomm or Robotics Chapter, where they will continue to be exposed to a specialised variety of STEAM topics.

What are the timings for our CCA sessions?

Typically, we hold CCA sessions twice a week.
Monday sessions last from 4:00pm to 6:00pm (2 hours).
Friday sessions last from 3:00pm to 6:00pm (3 hours).

Do take note that club members are expected to devote more time during competition seasons or public events. Not to fret, however, as we try our best to make sure our CCA does not heavily interfere with our academics and personal lives.

What qualities are we looking for in our applicants?

We are mainly looking for bright-eyed youths who possess a vested interest in programming and engineering. If you enjoy solving problems, possess a hardy and resilient mindset, and are excellent at cooperating with others, consider yourself accepted, for you are exactly who we are looking for.

Though applicants with skilled aptitudes in programming, algorithmic design, engineering, or robot construction are certainly a welcome surprise, we would like to emphasise that no prior experience in robotics or programming is required. In the end, it's your personality that counts.

What's it like being a STEAM Collective member?

Honestly? Stressful at times. Most of us have spent several painstaking hours in the final weeks leading up to a competition or an event (like our open house!), and a lot of effort is funnelled into planning and executing things. Not to mention, we often deal with time and manpower constraints, which can hinder us severely.

Yet, the payoff of these experiences makes the grinding all the more worthwhile. The satisfaction of stepping up onto a podium to receive an award, of seeing an event being carried out successfully without a hitch, of the joyous laughter and cheers that we share with each other, only strengthens our identity as a collective, and as a group of people who can manage to pull off great things, even when the going gets tough.

We often tire ourselves, but as the old adage goes, no pain, no gain. Being a STEAM Collective member allows us to learn a multitude of skills that would otherwise never be taught in a traditional classroom. Though we sometimes disagree with each other, we resolve our arguments, tightening the bonds that make us the CCA that we are. Granted, being a STEAM Collective member is hard work, but there's no question that it's rewarding beyond words.
